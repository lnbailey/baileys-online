About the Baileys

DAD

Robert Jack Bailey was born in Kingsport, TN. He's called Bob, but we call him Dad. He also goes by Red River Bob down at Station Inn. He retired form the airforce in 1992. Then retired from the Post Office in 2002. He is an amazing guitar player and a great dad.

MOM

Mary E. Slemp was born in Big Stone Gap, VA. She is known by many as Mother Mary, due to her taking in of strays and general goodness of her heart. She worked at Eastmas Kodak (now TN Eastman Co) in Kingsport until she married our dad. She started having babies and didn't know how to stop (LOL). She is the best mom you could ever ask for.

WAYNE

Wayne Alan Bailey was born in OK when our parents were stationed there. He was known by his cycle friends as Wayne Chung. He was a master carpenter and restored vintage bicycles and loved to restore or build Honda motorcycles. He was also a great guitar and bass player. We lost him in 2015 but he is one of those people you could never forget. A true one-of-a-kind. The world just isn't that same without him.

LINDA

Linda Nell Bailey (Hall), aka Roo, was born in Bermuda while our parents were stationed there. She is married to Gene Hall. They have two daughters, Rachel and Jasmine. And, now have a grandaughter (by Rachel) name Daniella. A true big sister and amazing woman.

SUSAN

Susan Marie Bailey (Johns), middle child. Forget Martha Stewart. She and her husband, Jim, grow grapes for jam and juice, pecans for pies and just yum, sugar cane for cane syrup along with all kinds of veggies. She also designs and makes custom quilts by hand. She has quite a creative and beautiful portfolio. They have two daughters, Mari Ellen and Jessica and son, Will.

CAROL

Carol Jean Bailey (Lockert) is, contrary to popular belief, not the baby. She has two sons, Ethan and Ryan and is married to Perry Lockert. She has another son, Ryan (step) since marrying Perry in 2005. Carol is an artistic person with a beautiful soprano voice. She was a star in our theatre community in high school as well as singing in the choir.
